About

Builder by nature,
developer by trade.

It started with wanting to build worlds. I studied design for virtual theater and games, expecting to end up crafting imaginary places. Instead, I discovered I liked building real things even more.

After four years at Louwman, where I learned how software works at scale, I found my way to Tired of Cancer. As lead mobile developer, I build and maintain Untire — an app that helps people recover from cancer. It’s the kind of work that reminds you why you got into this.

Along the way, I became something of an AI-assisted development power user. I’ve built text-to-speech pipelines, automated workflows, and shipped more code with AI as co-pilot than most people write by hand. Not because it replaces thinking — but because it lets me spend more time on the interesting parts.

I’m at my best in the first 80% — the architecture, the exploration, the “what if we tried this?” I’ll finish the last 20% too, but that’s where I need good coffee and occasionally a deadline.
